LA's January Wildfires are Rare Yet Expected

By Brooke Miller

The Palisades wildfire is the most destructive fire in Los Angeles History destroying property from Malibu to Santa Monica. The fire has expanded to 17,000 acres remaining mostly uncontained.

Usually LA reports zero fires within the first three months of the year. Cold temperatures and increased precipitation normally gaurantee the county a fire free winter. This year, the rain did not come.

World Resources Institute

The lack of moisture combined with strong, dry winds is a large cause of the sudden increase in recent fire activity.

Climate change is only making the situation worse for numerous reasons:

  • Wildfires are more frequent and intense worldwide.
  • The hot, dry conditions result in extended and more severe fire seasons.
  • Bigger areas are more prone to wildfires.
